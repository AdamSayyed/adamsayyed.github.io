About

I am a Meyerhoff Scholar and a junior at the University of Maryland, Baltimore County (UMBC), pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science with a specialization in Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence. I focus on building end-to-end ML systems, designing models, engineering data pipelines, and deploying production-ready solutions across finance, geospatial analytics, and scientific computing. My work combines machine learning research with strong software engineering skills to deliver scalable, reliable, and domain-focused AI systems.

Background

I have gained hands-on experience developing and deploying machine learning solutions through a combination of research and industry internships. At Sandia National Laboratories, I engineered ML pipelines and integrated large language models with geospatial analytics software, improving classification accuracy on field data and creating robust multi-object tracking systems. At UMBC, I designed a multimodal neural architecture for stock-movement prediction, combining time-series financial indicators with transformer-based sentiment embeddings from fine-tuned FinBERT and RoBERTa models. This involved building end-to-end data pipelines, preprocessing, feature engineering, and aligning news sentiment with market data to generate actionable insights.

In prior research at the University of Iowa, I implemented and optimized seq2seq transformers for German-English translation, benchmarking performance against state-of-the-art models. At the FDA, I developed interactive R Shiny modules linked to dosing-model computations, including dynamic UI updates, error handling, input validation, and automated reporting.

Beyond ML, I have a strong software engineering background, with experience building full-stack applications, scalable backend systems, and low-level architectures in Python, C/C++, JavaScript, and assembly.

High-Performance IPC with POSIX Threads
